Central Texas Forecaster Consensus

TV + Troy Kimmel consensus for Austin / Round Rock / IH-35 corridor

Local forecasters from KVUE, KXAN, FOX 7, CBS Austin, Waco’s KWTX/KXXV, and Troy Kimmel expect a wetter pattern beginning tonight, with rain and storm chances increasing Wednesday through Friday.

Rain chances are about 20–30% today, rising to 30–40% Wednesday, 40–50% Thursday, and 50–70% Friday, then easing a bit Saturday and Sunday. Typical rainfall where storms form should run from less than 0.05 inch today to about 0.05–0.10 inch Wednesday, 0.10–0.20 inch Thursday, and 0.20–0.35 inch Friday. The most likely 7-day total for the IH-35 corridor is about 0.75 to 1.00 inch, with locally higher amounts possible in heavier storms. Temperatures start warm, with highs in the low 90s today, then trend to upper 80s Thursday and mid 80s Friday under cloud cover and rain, before slightly warmer readings return over the weekend.
